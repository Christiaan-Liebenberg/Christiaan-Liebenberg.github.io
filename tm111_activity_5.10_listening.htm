 1930s - Swing & Big Band Top 

Video B02: Glen Miller's 'In the Mood', part of a selection of 1930s, Big Band performances.
 1940s - Bebop Top 

Video B03: Bud Powell and his quintent play "Bouncin' with Bud", the first track on Bud's "The Amazing Bud Powell" album which showcases bebop's melodic complexity during improvizations.
 1950s - Cool Top 

Video B04: Miles Davis and his sextet play "So What", the first track on his "Kind of Blue" album, considered by many as his masterpiece, epitomizes jazz's "Cool Era" creating musical "space" between phrases.
 1960s - Modal & Latin Influences Top 

Video B05: Joao Gilberto, Stan Getz (and featuring Antonio Carlo Jobim), recorded "Getz / Gilberto" in 1964. It beautifully represents influence of Latin & Brazillian music on the jazz genre.
 1970s - Avant Garde Top 

Video B06: Herbie Hancock's "Crossings" album was released in 1972 where jazz artists experimented with newly electronic instruments, melodic atonality and complex polyrhythms

Video B07: This You Tube Playlist has over 100 jazz songs from the 1980s, 1990s and 2000s
